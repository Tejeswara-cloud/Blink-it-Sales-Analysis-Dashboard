# Blink-it-Sales-Analysis-Dashboard
Blinkit Sales Analysis Dashboard

Your dashboard gives a full business overview of Blinkit sales performance using KPIs, outlet analysis, item analysis, and customer rating insights. Basically, corporate people staring at colorful rectangles pretending they control chaos. Still, this one is actually clean and useful.

Dashboard Overview

This dashboard is designed to analyze:

Total Sales Performance
Average Sales Value
Number of Items Sold
Customer Ratings
Outlet Performance
Product Category Performance
Fat Content Analysis
Outlet Size & Location Impact
KPI Cards (Top Metrics)
Total Sales: 1.20M

Represents the overall revenue generated from all products sold across outlets.

Average Sales: 140.99

Shows the average revenue per transaction/item.

Number of Items: 9K

Total quantity of products sold.

Average Rating: 4

Indicates overall customer satisfaction rating. Surprisingly decent. Humanity occasionally approves of grocery delivery.

Outlet Establishment Trend (Top Right Line Chart)

This chart shows sales performance based on outlet establishment years.

Key Insights:
Peak sales happened around 2018 with approximately 205K sales.
Sales remained relatively stable after 2020.
Older outlets continue contributing consistently.
Business Meaning:

The company experienced strong expansion and performance growth around 2018, suggesting successful outlet scaling and customer adoption.

Fat Content Analysis (Donut Chart)

Compares sales between:

Low Fat Products
Regular Fat Products
Observation:

Regular fat products contribute higher sales compared to low-fat products.

Business Insight:

Customers still choose taste over health. Civilization remains committed to cheese and fried things.

Item Type Analysis (Bar Chart)

Shows sales contribution by product categories.

Top Performing Categories:
Fruits & Vegetables
Snack Foods
Household Items
Frozen Foods
Dairy Products
Lowest Performing:
Seafood
Breakfast Items
Starchy Foods
Business Insight:

Daily essentials and snack products dominate customer demand.

Outlet Size Analysis

Compares sales across outlet sizes:

Small
Medium
High
Observation:

Medium-sized outlets contribute the highest sales share.

Insight:

Medium outlets appear more operationally efficient and customer accessible.

Outlet Location Analysis

Analyzes sales across Tier locations.

Results:
Tier 3 outlets generate highest sales.
Followed by Tier 2.
Tier 1 contributes comparatively lower sales.
Business Insight:

Smaller cities and towns show stronger demand growth for quick-commerce services.

Outlet Type Performance Table

Compares outlet types using:

Total Sales
Number of Items
Average Sales
Ratings
Item Visibility
Best Performer:

Supermarket Type 1

Highest total sales
Highest number of items sold
Insight:

Larger supermarket-style outlets are the strongest revenue drivers.

Filters Available

The left-side panel allows filtering by:

Outlet Location Type
Outlet Size
Item Type

This makes the dashboard interactive and useful for detailed business analysis.

Overall Conclusion

The dashboard successfully provides:

Sales trend monitoring
Customer preference insights
Outlet performance evaluation
Product category analysis
Regional sales comparison

It helps management make data-driven decisions related to:

Inventory planning
Outlet expansion
Product strategy
Regional targeting

Honestly, for a student project this is pretty solid. Most dashboards either look like a spaceship control panel designed by sleep-deprived interns or a rainbow exploded in Excel. Yours is readable, balanced, and business-focused.
